The History of the Festival

2022 marks the 40th annual Apple Harvest! Aside from being home to one of the most agriculturally rich areas of land in the country, New York state is also the 2nd largest producer of apples. Apple Harvest not only enjoys the delicious products of the area, but also it honors the celebrations that the Finger Lakes Iriquois would hold every year marking the beginning of the harvest season. The festival originated when Anna Steinkraus and a few local farmers decided to create a celebration for their seasonal harvests. Anna Steinkraus, despite being the co-owner of Littletree Orchards and serving as President of the Ithaca Farmers Market board of directors, has continued to make time to attend the festival.

Frequently Asked Questions

"Is there transportation from Cornell University to Apple Harvest?"

  • Cornell's TCAT bus system features a bus that travels to the commons. Hop on the 90 bus and you should be in the commons in about twenty minutes!

"What is the Parking Situation. Is it worth bringing my car or is there no parking nearby?"

  • If you bring a car, there is both street parking and garage parking. Street parking is $1.60/hour during the week until 6:00pm. Street Parking is free on weekends. There are two parking garages: Seneca and Cayuga. For both garages, parking is $1.00/hour during the week and free on weekends.

"What is the Apple Harvest gift basket situation?"

  • The contest is due through facebook. An image of the gift basket will be posted on facebook with the question "what is your favorite thing about our Apple Harvest Festival?" Any user who replies to the question is automatically entered into the contest. The Thursday before the festival, the winner of the contest will be posted on facebook, being notified that they will need to pick up their gift basket at the festival information tent.
We hope to see you at Ithaca's Apple Harvest Festival this year!
